The Pendulum Bench

Two pendulums run side by side — a clean swing and one with a hooked knot that drifts into a faint double pendulum. Then measure g yourself from a length and a stopwatch time, the same way we did in the lab.

Two pendulums, twenty swings

Each pendulum leaves a fading trace, like a slow oscilloscope. The clean pendulum traces one sharp arc. The hooked-knot pendulum traces almost the same arc — but watch the bob: the little hook at the top adds a second hinge, so the path smears slightly and never quite repeats. Both stop after 20 full oscillations. The measured period sits a touch above the ideal T₀ = 2π√(l/g) on purpose: a 22° release is past the small-angle limit, so the finite-amplitude correction lengthens it by ~0.6%.

Type in a wire length, how many swings you counted, and the total time on the stopwatch. The bench works backwards to the gravity those numbers imply — exactly the calculation behind our experiment. Mass is along for the ride: it changes the ball, not the result.
